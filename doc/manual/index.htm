  What is Valyria Tear?

   Valyria Tear is an open-source single-player medieval-fantasy 2D J-RPG based on an extended Hero of Allacrost engine and inspired by classic console RPGs.

   The player incarnates Bronann, a young boy forced to take part into the struggle for the possession of a mysterious crystal. This powerful artifact will lead him to discover the actual threat set upon his world, but also courage and love.

   The game has all the features you can expect from JRPG classics: entertaining story development, colourful map exploration, active side-view battles, character management, puzzles... It is also translated in several languages.

  The main characters

Bronann

Gender: Male

Age: 17

   Bronann is a young boy who has always lived peacefully in the village of Layna as far as he remembers. Despite his age, the protective nature of his parents which have left him a bit naive, he is a helpful and willing lad, and is strongly trying to do for the best of his closings.

   Bronann is the main hero of this story and you will incarnate him at the beginning of the game. He is also be the strongest support in battles. His skills are a balanced mix between attacks and defensive spells.

Kalya

Gender: Female

Age: 16

   Kalya is a very handsome, yet very proud young woman who is living in the same village than Bronann since a few years with her father Herth and her brother Orlinn.

   While she is strong-tempered, she doesn't want to attract the attention or seem out of place. Also, Herth has taught her the art of battle and magic since she was little and she can become a master in protective spells, and fearsome attacks. Later, she will discover the true essence and the origins of her powers...

Sylve

Gender: Female

Age: 21

   Sylve is a reknown thief living in the north, past the Layna Region. Her thirst of theft is endless and she's gathered the most valuables possible for a secret purpose.

   Sylve is a seasoned and frank warrior, using cunning ways to get past her enemies defences and reach their weaknesses. Her blunt humour also hides a true-hearted woman who only hope for better tomorrows and revenge...

Thanis

Gender: Male

Age: 24

   Thanis is a fearsome warrior, leader of the Rebel Army and the heir of the Ancient civilization. He's been raised and trained to become the armed fist of the Rebels, and fulfill every hopes in it.

   Thanis is also very proud of himself and will show it whenever he can. On the other hand, he will never show the pain his parents left when they abandonned him a long time ago, and prefers to put all his anger on the Lord's troops that plague his land...

Orlinn

Age: 12

   Orlinn is Kalya's younger brother and Herth's son. He's a joyful and jolly lad, who likes to do tricks to the others for fun.

Herth

Age: 45

   Herth is father of Kalya and Orlinn, the strongest fighter in their village and thus, the main watcher of its gate.

Carson

Age: 38

   Carson is the father of Bronann and husband of Malta. He is a former soldier of the Royal Army, before the arrival of the Lord and his dark soldiers. He's retired with his family in the village of Layna in the hope to find peace, and a safe ground for him and his family.

Malta

Age: 42

   Malta is the mother of Bronann and wife of Carson.

  Basic Keys

   When running Valyria Tear for the first time, a window will prompt you to select a language with the UP and DOWN keyboard arrows.

   Selecting the language will be done by pushing any other key. Don't worry, you'll be able to select a new language in the Options menu later on.

   Valyria Tear can also be played using a gamepad. In that case, selecting the language can be done with using the first gamepad axis or D-Pad, and selected with any standard buttons.

   Here are the game default key and their general purpose in the game:

  The main menu

   The main menu will show you the following options: 'New Game', 'Load Game', and 'Quit'. You can change the selected menu option using the arrows and use the confirm key to enter a menu. Once the main menu is shown, you can push keyboard Escape key to quit the game.

   'New game' will trigger a new game. It is selected by default when no save game has been created.

   'Load game' will open the Load menu. This option is only enabled if one valid save game file is found. It is selected by default when one valid save game file is found.

   'Quit' will close the game application.

   A few options are available when you compile the game using the 'developer mode' option: 'Menu', 'Battle', and 'Shop'. Those menus will be placed between 'Options' and 'Quit'.

   'Menu' will open a debug party menu. The content and available characters in this party menu is configurable in the 'dat/debug/debug_menu.lua' script.

   'Battle' will open a debug battle. The content and available characters in this battle is configurable in the 'dat/debug/debug_battle.lua' script.

   'Shop' will open a debug party menu. The content and available characters in this party menu is configurable in the 'dat/debug/debug_shop.lua' script.

   The game version and compile time is also shown on the bottom-left part of the main menu.

  The options menu

   The 'Options' menu is available through the main menu when you select 'Options' or when you push the 'Escape' keyboard of the 'Quit' joystick key key while in game or in another menu. The options menu will let you change different game settings, such as Video, Audio, Language, Keyboard keys, or Joystick settings.

   Note that the language settings are disabled when you are not in the main menu.

The Video settings

 

   The video settings will let you change the current screen resolution, whether the game should run in fullscreen or windowed mode, the screen overall brightness, and the UI theme.

The Audio settings

   The audio settings will let you change the music and game sounds overall volume.

The Language settings

   The language settings will let you change the game in one of the available languages. By default, the game will be in English.

The Keyboard settings

   The keyboard settings will let you configure the game keys when playing with a keyboard. See Basic Keys for the default configuration.

The Joystick settings

   The joystick settings will permit to configure the first joystick plugged in. You can move there using the keyboard for instance, while selecting the joystick key with the joystick itself when configuring.
   You can also disable joystick input in case it is giving issues. Both the joystick (if enabled) and the keyboard can be used to play the game at the same time.

  The Load/Save menu

 

   This menu lets you select one of the 6 game slots available to save your progress. When selecting the load menu through the 'Load Game' option, you can only load a game. To save your game, you'll have to find save points and push the confirm key (Default: 'F') while the character you're controlling is within the save point to be able to save the game on any slot.

   When saving, the last save slot used will be selected.

   When moving through available slots with up and down keys, the given slot will report information about the currently active characters in the party, their main characteristics, the time played, and the money the party is currently owning. The save point general location and image are also displayed.

  In Game...

 

   This the main game mode. In this mode you incarnate one character usually centered on the map, and you can move him around using the arrow keys, you can also run by holding the Cancel Key (Default: 'D') and move around.
   You can also interact with save points, other characters, and certain hidden spots using the Confirm key (Default: 'F').

   In certain locations, minimaps are available (See image on the left) and can be toggled on/off using the Toggle Minimap key (Default: 'C'). In the minimap, several icon indicates interesting items, such as:

- Character location and direction.

- Save points: Used to save the game. See the Load/Save Menu.

- Layna statues: Restores the party HP/SP.

   The stamina bar: When moving around, mainly in dangerous areas, you will most probably encounter enemies wandering around and chasing your character if going close to them. When walking, you're moving slower than you're enemies, but when running, you will be able to escape them, at a cost:
The more your character run, the more the stamina will deplete. Once empty, you won't be able to run anymore. Also, you'll have to stop pressing the Running key (Default: 'D') for it to start filling again.

   If an enemy catches you when the stamina bar is low, an agility malus will be applied on your party at the beginning of the fight. The less full the bar is, the higher the malus will be.

   Last but not least, in friendly areas, where there are no enemies, the stamina bar will display an infinite symbol and disappear. This means you can run as much as you want in the corresponding location.
